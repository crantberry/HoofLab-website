Dr. Andy Hoofnagle, MD, PhD

Dr. Hoofnagleâ€™s laboratory focuses on research and development of novel biomarker assays that use mass spectrometry for the care of patients. Their research projects span the entire lifespan of a biomarker from discovery to application in clinical research to the development of robust, scalable methods for clinical care. Areas of interest include cancer, cardiovascular disease, doping in sports, nutrition, diabetes, metabolism, and dementia. Funding is currently provided by NIDDK, NIA, and the Partnership for Clean Competition. Partial equipment support is provided by Waters, Inc.

Lili Barahona-Carillo, Research Scientist 2

I am from Queretaro, Mexico but have grown up in Washington state. I enjoy spending time with my familia, dancing and traveling around Latin America. My career has joyfully taken many turns in a variety of industries. Including a winery analytical lab for quality assurance testing of commercial and premium brands, and the food safety and pesticide data sector running highly complex analysis of environmental products for pesticides, chemical residues, nutrients and elements.

I obtained my B.S. in Chemistry from CWU, where I worked on protein-protein interactions to elucidate interfaces involved in the development of the mouse neocortex. Additionally, I have a M.S. in Biomedical Diagnostics from ASU, and have a great interest in LCMS and GCMS instrumentation and their application to medical research. Currently I am a Research Scientist/Engineer 2 with the Hoofnagle lab where we research and develop analytical chemistry techniques to quantify proteins and small molecules in complex mixtures.
